Chunk 4 itself is the strongest chunk so far, and the failure-path testing is why. The happy path passing is expected: cert chain verifies (return code: 0), SAN is dc01.poc0.lab, and the LDAPS bind as svc-bind returned alice with result: 0 Success. Fine. The valuable part is that Claude deliberately captured both failure signatures — SAN mismatch (connecting by IP) and untrusted issuer (FQDN without the CA) — and noted that both surface as the same generic Can't contact LDAP server (-1) at the ldapsearch layer, with openssl being the tool that actually distinguishes them (verify error:num=62:hostname mismatch). That is exactly the insurance you wanted: when Chunk 5's containerized app fails to bind — and first attempts often do — you now know that a cert/trust failure and a SAN failure both look like a vague connection error, and you know openssl s_client is how you tell them apart from inside the container. Without that, you'd be staring at "can't contact server" unable to tell whether it's network, DNS, cert trust, or SAN. This is the kind of pre-work that turns a two-hour Chunk 5 debug into a ten-minute one.

One thing on the SAN that's quietly correct: the cert carries both dc01.poc0.lab and poc0.lab. The app must connect by dc01.poc0.lab (the name that matches), and that's what the ldaps_fqdn output hands to Chunk 5 — make sure the container app config uses that output and isn't hardcoded to an IP, or you'll reproduce the SAN-mismatch failure you just documented.
